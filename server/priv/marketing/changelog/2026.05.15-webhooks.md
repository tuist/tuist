You can now register HTTPS endpoints at the account level and subscribe each one to the Tuist events it cares about. Tuist signs every payload with HMAC-SHA256, sends it with `Tuist-Event-Id` / `Tuist-Event-Type` / `Tuist-Signature` headers, and retries failed deliveries on a 1m → 5m → 30m → 2h → 8h → 24h schedule. The current event catalog covers `test_case.created`, `test_case.updated`, `preview.created`, and `preview.deleted` — enough to drive the most common automations: post fresh builds into Notion, mirror flaky-test transitions into your incident tracker, or kick off downstream QA workflows the moment a preview is ready.

Each endpoint has its own detail page with a delivery chart, a searchable event log, and a per-event view that shows the exact request body, headers, and upstream response. Rotating the signing secret, editing the subscription list, or pausing deliveries is one click away.
